What is Theravada Buddhism?

The Theravada tradition is largely considered to be the "truest" form of Buddhism. Unlike the Mahayana traditions, the Theravadans take a more psychological and philosophical (as opposed to religious) approach to achieving liberation.

The Differences between Theravada and Buddhism

Enlightened are called Arhat Enlightened are called Bodhisattva

Common to Both Schools

  • Siddhartha Gautama was the first human Buddha
  • Meditation is the staple of skillful practice
  • The Nobe Eightfold Path is the way to unshakeable liberation of mind
